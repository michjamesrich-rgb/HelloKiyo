A kawaii “build‑your‑box” shopping spree powered by vibes

Target: female 12–24. Offer: themed Japanese assortment boxes with no individual item pricing on the storefront. Shopify (headless later) + custom frontend demo.

1) Executive summary

Hello Kiyo sells a playful Japanese discovery experience: customers choose a box size, browse cute categories, and build a “spree” without seeing item prices. Checkout is box price + shipping + optional gift wrap.

  • Core product: curated assortment box with customizable tiers.
  • Monetization: box ticket (product-only) + shipping + gift wrap add‑on.
  • Launch wedge: $50 “Kiyo’s Mini” curated box (bulk buy + repeatable fulfillment).

Key operating principles

  • No item pricing: the storefront sells the experience and category vibes, not SKU unit prices.
  • Shipping separate: destination + box size determines Standard/Expedited quote.
  • Inventory-free (except $50 curated): customizable tiers assume near-retail procurement.
  • Teen‑girl alignment: prioritize colorful, kawaii, low‑yen items first within each category.

3) Market, customer, and positioning

Who we win

  • Primary: girls/young women 12–24 who love kawaii culture (Sanrio, anime, stickers, snack TikTok).
  • Secondary: gift buyers (birthdays, back‑to‑school, holidays) and “Japan-curious” shoppers.
  • Psychographics: novelty seeking, aesthetic-first shopping, creator-driven discovery.

Positioning

  • Not a SKU store: this is an experience-led box with category vibes.
  • Game-like UX: missions/bonuses + fun visual feedback, not discounting.
  • Brand mascot: “Kiyo” anchors identity and content voice.
Competitive set: Japanese snack boxes, stationery subscriptions, influencer storefronts. Differentiator is choose-your-vibe customization + a merch/sponsorship flywheel.
